The Most Nuked Place on Earth

The Semipalatinsk Test Site or Semipalatinsk-21, also known as "The Polygon.

It was the primary testing venue for the Soviet Union's nuclear weapons. A total of 456 nuclear explosions were held at Semipalatinsk test site over four decades. The total explosive yield was 250 times the bomb that razed Hiroshima to the ground in 1945. It is located in Zhanasemey District, Abai Region, Kazakhstan, south of the valley of the Irtysh River

Around 1.6 million people living in the districts and cities of the Semipalatinsk region and the corresponding districts of the Pavlodar, East Kazakhstan and Karaganda regions are recognized as victims of nuclear tests conducted by the Soviet military-industrial complex from 1949 to 1989.
